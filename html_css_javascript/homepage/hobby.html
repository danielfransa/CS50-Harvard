My Hobby

One of my main interests today is software development, especially web development. I enjoy building applications using technologies like Ruby on Rails and JavaScript, and I find it very rewarding to see an idea turn into a working system. What motivates me the most is the process of solving problems and continuously improving my skills. Since I transitioned into technology after years working in industry, I bring a different perspective to development, focusing not only on code but also on efficiency, organization, and real-world usability. I like exploring how systems are structured, understanding backend logic, and gradually improving my frontend skills to create complete applications. I also enjoy learning about software quality, testing, and best practices, as I believe writing clean and maintainable code is just as important as making things work. For me, programming is not just a job or a field of study, but a long-term journey where I can constantly evolve and build meaningful solutions.

Another important interest in my life is music, especially playing the guitar. I see music as both a creative outlet and a way to relax after studying or working. I enjoy learning new chords, practicing rhythm, and understanding the fundamentals of music theory, which helps me play more confidently and independently. One of my goals is to become good enough to accompany songs naturally, without relying too much on tutorials, and to eventually help other beginners learn as well. Playing the guitar also teaches discipline and patience, since progress comes with consistent practice over time. I find it interesting how music and programming share similarities, as both require practice, pattern recognition, and gradual improvement. Whether I am practicing alone or thinking about teaching others in the future, music remains an important part of my routine and a hobby that I truly enjoy.

I also have a strong interest in continuous learning and personal development. Currently, I am studying at college and complementing my education with online courses and practical projects. I enjoy exploring different areas of technology, such as natural language processing, software architecture, and full stack development. What drives me is the idea of building a solid foundation that allows me to adapt and grow in the long term. I like experimenting with new tools, testing ideas, and gradually improving my understanding of how systems work. Beyond technical skills, I value organization, problem-solving, and consistency, as I believe these qualities make a big difference in any career. Learning is not something I see as temporary, but as a continuous process that is part of my daily life. This mindset helps me stay motivated and focused on achieving my goals in technology.
